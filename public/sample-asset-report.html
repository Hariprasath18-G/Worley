5. Known Constraints & Considerations

The following constraints have been identified by the asset team and should inform any transition pathway assessment:

  • CDU approaching end of design life (2033) — major reinvestment decision required within 2–3 years. Without investment, throughput must reduce by ~30% from 2030.
  • Steam cracker furnace replacement — estimated £80M capital requirement within 3–5 years to maintain ethylene production. Deferral beyond 2029 creates safety risk.
  • Forties Pipeline System dependency — North Sea crude production declining; feedstock diversification or alternative use must be evaluated.
  • Scottish Government hydrogen strategy — Grangemouth has been identified as a potential anchor site for Scotland's hydrogen hub. Letters of interest received from two electrolyser OEMs.
  • Workforce transition sensitivity — 1,200 FTEs in a region with limited alternative industrial employment. Any transition must include a credible just-transition plan per Scottish Enterprise requirements.
  • CO₂ transport and storage — Acorn CCS project (St Fergus) is 180km north; pipeline connection feasibility study commissioned but not yet complete.
  • Legacy contamination in Zone C — remediation required regardless of pathway chosen. £45M provisioned but actual cost uncertain (range £30M–£70M based on Phase II assessment).
  • Adjacent land availability — 120 hectares of brownfield land adjacent to the complex, currently unused, suitable for new-build facilities (hydrogen electrolysis, biofuel processing, or logistics).
